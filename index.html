Gheymeh (Persian Split Pea Stew)

Tomato-rich, warm spices, dried lime tang, and crispy potatoes on top.

Introduction

I chose Gheymeh because it feels like a “home-food” anchor in Persian cooking: familiar aroma, comforting texture, and a flavor you recognize immediately. The combination of tomato, yellow split peas, warm spices, and dried lime creates a balance of savory and tangy that is hard to beat. For me, it is also tied to family meals and gatherings, so it is not just something I eat, it is something I remember.

Traditional Gheymeh is made with beef. I prepare it with chicken because I do not eat beef, while keeping the rest of the recipe authentic in flavor and preparation.

Ingredients

For the stew

  • 450 g chicken breast, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 cup (200 g) yellow split peas
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil (or neutral cooking oil)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes (or 3–4 grated fresh tomatoes)
  • 2 dried limes (limoo amani), pierced with a fork
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cups (950 ml) water or chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional, for extra brightness)

Traditional Gheymeh uses beef. This version uses chicken as a personal dietary preference.

For the topping

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into thin matchsticks
  • Oil for frying (or baking/air-frying)
  • Pinch of salt

To serve

  • Steamed basmati rice
  • Optional: pickles, fresh herbs, or yogurt on the side

If dried limes are unavailable, you may add extra lemon juice at the end, but dried lime gives the most authentic flavor.

Step-by-step Directions

  1. Rinse and soak the split peas: Rinse under cool water until mostly clear. Soak for 20 to 30 minutes (optional).
  2. Par-cook the split peas: Simmer for about 15 minutes until slightly softened. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the onion: Heat oil and cook onion until golden and soft, about 8–10 minutes.
  4. Add spices: Stir in turmeric, cinnamon, pepper, and salt and cook for 30 seconds.
  5. Sear the chicken: Add chicken and cook 5 to 7 minutes until lightly browned.
  6. Build the tomato base: Add tomato paste, then crushed tomatoes, and cook for several minutes.
  7. Simmer: Add split peas and water or stock. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  8. Add dried limes: Pierce and add to the pot. Simmer 35 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Prepare the topping: Fry or bake potatoes until crisp and golden.
  10. Finish: Remove dried limes if desired. Adjust seasoning and add lemon juice if needed.
  11. Serve: Spoon over rice and top with crispy potatoes.

Tips for Success

  • Let the stew simmer longer for deeper flavor.
  • Use one dried lime if you prefer a milder tang.
  • Add water if too thick, or simmer uncovered if too thin.
  • Keep steps short and readable for ease while cooking.
