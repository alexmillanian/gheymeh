Gheymeh (Persian Split Pea Stew)

Tomato-rich, warm spices, dried lime tang, and crispy potatoes on top.

Serves: 4 Time: ~75 minutes Difficulty: Medium

Introduction

I chose Gheymeh because it feels like a “home-food” anchor in Persian cooking: familiar aroma, comforting texture, and a flavor you recognize immediately. The combination of tomato, yellow split peas, warm spices, and dried lime creates a balance of savory and tangy that is hard to beat. For me, it is also tied to family meals and gatherings, so it is not just something I eat, it is something I remember.

This webpage is designed so someone can cook while reading: ingredients are scannable, steps are clearly ordered, and images support what the finished dish should look like.

Ingredients

For the stew

  • 450 g chicken breast, cut into bite-size pieces

    • Traditional Gheymeh is made with beef. I substitute chicken because I do not eat beef, while keeping the rest of the recipe authentic in flavor and preparation.

  • 1 cup (200 g) yellow split peas
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil (or neutral cooking oil)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes (or 3–4 grated fresh tomatoes)
  • 2 dried limes (limoo amani), pierced with a fork
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cups (950 ml) water or chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional, for extra brightness)

For the topping

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into thin matchsticks
  • Oil for frying (or baking/air-frying)
  • Pinch of salt

To serve

  • Steamed basmati rice
  • Optional: pickles, fresh herbs, or yogurt on the side

Note: If you cannot find dried limes, you can substitute a little extra lemon juice at the end, but dried lime gives the most classic flavor.

Step-by-step Directions

  1. Rinse and soak the split peas: Rinse the yellow split peas under cool water until the water runs mostly clear. Soak them in water for 20–30 minutes (optional, but helps them cook more evenly).
  2. Par-cook the split peas: Drain the peas. Add them to a pot with fresh water and simmer for about 15 minutes. They should soften slightly, not turn mushy. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the onion: In a large pot, heat 3 tbsp oil. Add the chopped onion and cook until golden and soft, about 8–10 minutes.
  4. Add spices: Stir in turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, and salt. Cook for 30 seconds to bloom the spices.
  5. Sear the chicken: Add chicken pieces and cook 5–7 minutes, stirring until the outside turns opaque and lightly browned.
  6. Build the tomato base: Stir in tomato paste and cook 1–2 minutes. Add crushed tomatoes and cook another 3–4 minutes.
  7. Simmer: Add the par-cooked split peas and 4 cups water/stock. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  8. Add dried limes: Pierce dried limes with a fork, then add them to the pot. Simmer uncovered or partially covered for 35–45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Make the potato topping: Fry potato matchsticks in hot oil until crisp and golden, then drain on paper towels and salt lightly. (Alternative: bake or air-fry until crisp.)
  10. Finish and taste: Remove the dried limes if the flavor is strong enough. Adjust salt, and add lemon juice if you want more brightness. The stew should be thick and spoonable.
  11. Serve: Spoon Gheymeh over basmati rice and top with crispy potatoes. Enjoy while hot.

Quick food safety note

Cook chicken until fully done (no pink, juices run clear). Refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours and reheat until steaming hot.

Tips for Success

  • Want deeper flavor? Let it simmer a little longer so the sauce thickens.
  • Dried lime intensity: Start with 1 lime if you are unsure, then add the second.
  • Texture control: If it gets too thick, add a splash of water. If too thin, simmer uncovered.
